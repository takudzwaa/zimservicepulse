# ZimServicePulse – 3-Minute Pitch Script & Demo Choreography

All numbers below are computed live by the app from the official dataset
(full national scope, Jan–Jun 2026). If the dataset changes, re-read them
from the dashboard — never quote stale figures.

**Setup before you're called up:** app running at `localhost:8501`, all
filters reset (use the sidebar **Reset all filters** button), **Offline map
mode ON if venue Wi-Fi is unreliable**, browser at the top of the page.

---

## Part 1 — Problem (30 seconds)

> "Every month, Zimbabwean councils receive tens of thousands of citizen
> service requests — water, roads, waste, licensing, documentation. Right
> now, 264,572 requests are in this dataset and nearly one in five —
> 51,571 — is unresolved. Managers know there's a backlog. What they can't
> see is **where** the pressure is concentrated and **what to do first**.
> ZimServicePulse fixes that."

*(Screen: Step 1 — problem banner and the four KPI cards are already
visible. Don't touch anything yet.)*

## Part 2 — Live demo (90 seconds)

Follow the 4 steps top to bottom. Exact clicks:

1. **Overview (~15s).** Point at the KPIs: satisfaction 3.53/5, only 73.4%
   resolved on time. Point at the map: "Every bubble is a district — size is
   demand, colour is pressure. The red one is **Chinhoyi**, our highest-
   pressure district."

2. **Explore (~35s).** In the sidebar, set **Settlement type → Rural**.
   Say: "Watch everything recalculate — KPIs, map, rankings." Click the
   **Service categories** tab: "Waste management tops the rural backlog."
   Click the **Channels** tab: "Digital channels resolve on time more often
   than walk-in — that's a channel strategy hiding in plain sight."

3. **Insights (~20s).** Scroll to Step 3: "These cards are generated by a
   transparent rules engine — every number is computed from the filtered
   data. Nothing is hardcoded." Read one card aloud, e.g. the urgent-case
   concentration card.

4. **Actions (~20s).** Scroll to Step 4: "And this is the payoff —
   prioritised, location-aware actions. A daily escalation review for
   **Harare Urban, Bulawayo North and Marondera**, which hold 28% of the
   urgent backlog. A waste task-force for **Bulawayo Central, Chinhoyi and
   Gweru**." Click **Download insight & action summary** — "one click and
   this becomes a briefing document."

   Then click **Reset all filters** so the screen returns to the national
   view for questions.

## Part 3 — Impact & next steps (60 seconds)

> "Three things make this decision-ready rather than just a dashboard.
> First, honest metrics: satisfaction and on-time rates are weighted by
> request volume, so a five-request ward can't distort the national picture.
> Second, a transparent pressure score — 50% backlog, 25% late resolution,
> 25% low satisfaction — so officials can defend every ranking. Third, every
> insight maps to a named action in a named place.
>
> Next steps: connect live channel data instead of monthly extracts, add
> district drill-downs for field supervisors, and pilot with two councils —
> starting with the highest-pressure districts this tool already identified.
>
> ZimServicePulse helps local authorities see exactly where public services
> are under pressure — and gives them clear, prioritised actions to improve
> citizen experience."

## Q&A crib sheet

- **Where do the numbers come from?** Only `01_public_service_requests.csv`
  (720 rows, Jan–Jun 2026, 20 districts). Zero hardcoded figures; the footer
  says so and `BRIEF.md` §6 documents each formula.
- **Why weighted averages?** Rows are aggregates; naive means would let tiny
  districts skew national KPIs.
- **What if there's no internet?** Sidebar → Offline map mode renders the
  bundled province outlines; everything else is already local. Offline
  reinstall: `pip install --no-index --find-links vendor/wheels -r requirements.txt`.
- **How are insights generated?** Five deterministic rules (backlog leader,
  channel gap, urgent concentration, satisfaction floor, slow resolution)
  with documented thresholds in `insights.py`. AI/ML ranking is a roadmap
  item, not a claim.
